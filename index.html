Hi, I’m Florence! As a child in a philosophy lecture, I never thought I’d end up in the lab — but my obsession with p-zombies led me through neurobiology and computational neuroscience, into the world of neural interfaces, cybernetics design, and cognitive augmentation.

I make all sorts of doodads. From memory enhancing wearables, brain-to-brain communication implants, and biomimetic prosthetic limbs, to sundial sculptures, smart furniture, and artificial senses — I love bringing insights from one field into another.

My research interests lie in multiscale neural recording and edge ML as a compression method for wireless BCI. I’ve been extremely fortunate to work alongside some of the best teams in neurotechnology and nanoscience, with contributions to multiple implant studies and novel neural-imaging modalities — from PCBs and neural decoding for BrainGate and Synchron’s clinical trials, to beamforming and spline interpolation for the Butterfly Garden.

An avid 3D-printing fan, and a rabid TTRPG one. Let’s chat!

Biomimetic prosthetics

Open Bionics

// HARDWARE ENGINEER · 2014–2019

I was an open-source hardware engineer designing 3D-printed bionic arms. I implemented metamaterial joints and infill patterns to reduce motor weight and torque, full wrist and thumb actuation via custom harmonic gearing, interpolated haptic proprioception and force feedback, and improved adaptive grasping via biomimetic tendon-routing structures. My work spanned multiple prototypes and generations of the Ada Arm and its electronics.

BrainGate

// NEUROENGINEER · BRAINGATE TEAM · 2015–2021

A neuroengineer prototyping neural dust for brain-to-brain communication: FPGA-accelerated decoders for networks of wireless electrodes, neuromorphic RF communication algorithms, and microscale neural recording hardware. PRJ_04

Endovascular neuroelectronics

Synchron

// SR. NEUROELECTRONICS ENGINEER · 2022–2023

I was an electrical and embedded systems engineer designing endovascular neural interfaces for patients with ALS. I developed Class III implant firmware including secure data transfer and embedded data logging, plus hardware for cloud-based nonstationarity compensation and real-time motor decoding. I also built models of signal propagation through custom electrode interfaces to drive material selection and fabrication. My work is represented in the clinical COMMAND trial.

PRJ_05

Ultrasound-on-chip · 4Catalyzer

Butterfly Network

// EMBEDDED ML ENGINEER · 4CATALYZER · 2019–2022

A researcher at a medtech consortium developing novel algorithms, devices, and models for miniaturized brain and biomedical imaging. At 454 Bio, I built physics-informed data-augmentation models for in-vivo protein quantification, low-field MRI, and ultrasonic intracranial-pressure monitoring, plus sensor-fusion for multimodal seizure detection. At Tesseract Health & Quantum-Si, I engineered hardware-accelerated imaging pipelines and classifiers for automated detection of diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma, and Alzheimer's. At Hyperfine & Liminal Sciences, I developed a transcranial-Doppler suite for point-of-care MRI — MEMS ultrasound driver boards and beamsteering for vessel localization. At Butterfly Network, I designed wearable whole-brain ultrasonic neuromodulation: CUDA beamforming kernels, driver boards, and Jetson firmware. My work is distributed throughout the Butterfly Garden.
